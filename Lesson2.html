Information: Situational Greetings

Business first meeting

Business cards are exchanged. ("Hajimemashite.")

New neighbors

Introduce yourself and give a small gift (towel, soap, sweets). Say Honno kimochi desu.

Grammar Explanation

1. Kore / Sore / Are — demonstratives that function as nouns. "Kore" near speaker, "Sore" near listener, "Are" far from both.

Sore wa zasshi desu ka?

→ Is that a magazine?

Kore wa kagi desu.

→ This is a key.

2. Kono / Sono / Ano + N — modify nouns. "Kono N" near speaker, "Sono N" near listener, "Ano N" far from both.

Kono nōto wa watashi no desu. → This notebook is mine.

Ano kata wa donata desu ka? → Who is that person?

3. Sō desu / Sō ja arimasen — affirmative/negative responses in noun sentences. "Hai, sō desu." / "Iie, sō ja arimasen."

Sore wa techō desu ka? → Is that a pocket notebook?

Hai, sō desu. / Iie, chigaimasu.

4. S1 ka, S2 ka — choose between alternatives; answer with the chosen sentence.

Kore wa zasshi ka shinbun desu ka? → Is this a magazine or a newspaper?

Zasshi desu. → It is a magazine.

5. N1 no N2 — N1 explains what N2 is about or who owns N2.

Kore wa kuruma no zasshi desu. → This is a magazine of cars.

Kore wa watashi no zasshi desu. → This is my magazine.

Are wa dare no hon desu ka? → Whose book is that?

Kono kasa wa anata no desu ka? → Is this umbrella yours?

Iie, watashi no ja arimasen. → No, it's not mine.

6. Sō desu ka — used to acknowledge new information.

Kono kaban wa anata no desu ka? → Is this bag yours?

Iie, chigaimasu. Tanabi-san no desu. → No, it's Mr. Tanabi's.

Sō desu ka. → I see.

Translation Activity 2

Exercise A: Sentence Pattern

1.

This is a dictionary.

Kore wa jisho desu.

2.

This is a book on computers.

Kore wa konpyūtā no hon desu.

3.

That is my umbrella.

Are wa watashi no kasa desu.

4.

This umbrella is mine.

Kono kasa wa watashi no desu.

Example Sentences

1.

Is this a telephone card? → Yes, it is.

Hai, sō desu.

2.

Is this a notebook? → No, it's not. It's a pocket notebook.

Iie, chigaimasu. Techō desu.

3.

What is that? → This is a business card.

Kore wa meishi desu.

4.

Is this a business card or a telephone card? → It's a business card.

Meishi desu.

5.

Whose coffee is that? → It's Mr. Tanabi's coffee.

Tanabi-san no kōhii desu.

6.

Is this chocolate yours? → No, it's not mine.

Iie, watashi no ja arimasen.

7.

Whose magazine is this? → It's mine.

Watashi no desu.

Exercise B: Conversation — This is just a Token

Ramos:

Yes. Who is it?

Hai. Donata desu ka?

Ibarra:

I am Dante Ibarra from (apartment) 583.

Watashi wa go-happyaku hachijū san-gō no Dante Ibarra desu.

Ibarra:

Hello. I am Dante Ibarra. How do you do? It is nice to meet you.

Konnichiwa. Watashi wa Dante Ibarra desu. Hajimemashite. Dōzo yoroshiku.

Ramos:

The pleasure's mine.

Kochira koso.

Ibarra:

Hmm, this is a little something...

Etto, honno kimochi desu…

Ramos:

Oh, thank you. What is it?

Ā, arigatō. Nan desu ka?

Ibarra:

It's chocolate. Please.

Chokorēto desu. Dōzo.

Ramos:

Thank you very much.

Dōmo arigatō gozaimasu.
