How to Write an Address

Address Example:

〒658-0063

Hyōgo-ken Kōbe-shi Chūō-ku Sannomiya 1-23

Kōbe-haitsu 405-gō

Labels: postal zip code, city, ward, town, district, building name, apartment number.

Grammar Explanation: Ima-ji-fun/pun desu — To express time, use -ji (o’clock) and -fun/-pun (minutes).
-fun is used after 2, 5, 7, 9; -pun is used after 1, 3, 4, 6, 8, 10. 1, 6, 8, 10 are read ippun, roppun, happun, juppun.

Ima nan-ji desu ka. → What time is it now?

Hachi-ji juppun desu. → It’s eight ten.

Nyūyōku wa ima nan-ji desu ka. → In New York, what time is it now?

Gozen go-ji desu. → It’s 5 a.m.

V–masu — A verb with masu works as a predicate and makes the sentence polite.

Watashi wa mainichi benkyō-shimasu. → I study every day.

V–masu / V–masen / V–mashita / V–masendeshita — Used to express habitual, future, or past actions.

Maiasa roku-ji ni okimasu. → I wake up at six every morning.

Kinō benkyō-shimashita ka? → Did you study yesterday?

Hai, benkyō-shimashita. → Yes, I did.

N(time) ni V — When a verb denotes a momentary action, ni marks the time.

Go-ji han ni okimasu. → I get up at five thirty.

N1 kara N2 made — kara = from; made = until.

Ku-ji kara go-ji made hatarakimasu. → I work from nine to five.

Ōsaka kara Tōkyō made san-jikan kakarimasu. → It takes three hours from Osaka to Tokyo.

N1 to N2 — Connects two nouns in coordinate relation.

Ginkō no yasumi wa doyōbi to nichiyōbi desu. → The bank is closed on Saturday and Sunday.

Sentence Ending: ne — Ne adds feeling, sympathy, or agreement.

Mainichi jū-ji made benkyō-shimasu. Taihen desu ne. → I study until 10 p.m. That must be hard.

Activity 4

Exercise A: Sentences

1.

It is four o’clock now.

Ima yo-ji desu.

2.

I work from nine to five.

Watashi wa ku-ji kara go-ji made hataraiteimasu.

3.

I get up at six in the morning.

Watashi wa asa roku-ji ni okimasu.

4.

I studied last night.

Kinou no yoru benkyou shimashita.

Example Sentences

1.

What time is it? → It is ten past two.

Ima nan-ji desu ka? → Ni-ji juu-pun desu.

2.

From what time is it now in New York? → It is ten past twelve at night.

Nyūyōku wa ima nan-ji desu ka? → Yoru juu-ni-ji juu-pun desu.

3.

What time do you go to bed every night? → I go to bed at nine o’clock.

Anata wa maiban nan-ji ni nemasu ka? → Ku-ji ni nemasu.

4.

Do you work on Saturdays? → No, I don’t.

Doyōbi ni hataraiteimasu ka? → Iie, hataraiteimasen.

5.

Did you study yesterday? → No, I didn’t.

Kinō benkyou shimashita ka? → Iie, shimasen deshita.

6.

What is your telephone number? → It is 045-5789.

Anata no denwa bangō wa nan desu ka? → 045-5789 desu.

Exercise B: Conversation

104:

Hello, this is Ishida of the 104 Service.

Moshi-moshi, 104 sābisu no Ishida desu.

Naraya:

Could you tell me the phone number of the ABC Art Museum, please?

ABC Āto Myūjiamu no denwa bangō o oshiete kudasai.

104:

The ABC Art Museum? Certainly.

ABC Āto Myūjiamu desu ka? Mochiron desu.

Record:

The number is 0797-38-5432.

Bangō wa 0797-38-5432 desu.

Staff:

Hello, ABC Art Museum.

Moshi-moshi, ABC Āto Myūjiamu desu.

Naraya:

Excuse me. What are your opening hours?

Sumimasen. Hiruma no kaijō jikan wa nan-ji kara nan-ji made desu ka?

Staff:

We are open from nine to four.

Ku-ji kara yo-ji made desu.

Naraya:

Which day are you closed?

Heijitsu wa yasumi desu ka?

Staff:

We are closed on Mondays.

Getsuyōbi wa yasumi desu.

Naraya:

Thank you very much.

Dōmo arigatō gozaimasu.
