Kaiwa (Conversation)

Person A:

どうもすみません。

Dōmo sumimasen. (Thank you)

Shop Assistant:

チリソースですか？

Chiri-sōsu desu ka? (Chili sauce?)

Grammar Explanation

1. ～が あります／います — Used to indicate the existence of things or people.
あります for inanimate, います for animate.

コンピューターが あります。 → There is a computer.

ねこが います。 → There is a cat.

2. ～に N が あります／います — Indicates a location.

わたしのへやに つくえが あります。 → There is a desk in my room.

3. N1 は N2 に あります／います — Used when topic is known.

東京ディズニーランドは ちばけん に あります。 → Tokyo Disneyland is in Chiba Prefecture.

4. N1 の N2 (position) — Used with position words like うえ, した, まえ, なか.

つくえのうえに しゃしんが あります。 → There is a picture on the desk.

5. N1 や N2 — Used to list examples.

はこのなかに てがみや しゃしんが あります。 → There are letters and pictures in the box.

Activity 9

Exercise A: Sentence Patterns

1.

Ms. Ramos is over there.

Ramos-san wa asoko ni imasu.

2.

There is a photo on the desk.

Tsukue no ue ni shashin ga arimasu.

3.

My family is in New York.

Watashi no kazoku wa Nyūyōku ni imasu.

4.

Tokyo Disneyland is in Chiba Prefecture.

Tōkyō Dizunīrando wa Chiba-ken ni arimasu.

Exercise B: Conversation

Tanabi:

Excuse me. Where is Yunyu store?

Sumimasen. Yunyu-sutō wa doko desu ka?

Woman:

Yunyu store? You see that white building over there? The store is in that building.

Yunyu-sutō desu ka? Ano shiroi biru no naka desu.

Tanabi:

I see. Thank you.

Sō desu ka. Arigatō gozaimasu.

Woman:

Not at all. Do you have chili sauce in this store?

Dō itashimashite. Kono mise ni chiri-sōsu wa arimasu ka?

Tanabi:

Excuse me, do you have chili sauce?

Sumimasen, chiri-sōsu wa arimasen ka?

Shop Assistant:

Yes. There is a spice corner on the right-hand side at the back. Chili sauce is on the second rack from the bottom.

Hai. Oku no migi gawa ni supaisu kōnā ga arimasu. Chiri-sōsu wa shita kara ni-dan-me ni arimasu.

Tanabi:

I see. Thank you.

Sō desu ka. Arigatō gozaimasu.
